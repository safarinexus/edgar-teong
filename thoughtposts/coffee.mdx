I wanted to make a post about this because coffee has become such a big hobby of mine and
an integral part of my life, and I thought what better time than now to document my journey 
in this hobby. It has been almost over a year now that I started making my own coffee at home 
and it has become something that I really enjoy. Alright, enough talk, let's dive into it. 

# Italy 
While I was away on exchange at the [University of St. Gallen](https://www.unisg.ch/), I took a trip down to Italy 
together with some friends, and we made stops at various cities throughout. First was Milan, 
then our second stop would be at Florence before heading to Rome, Sorento, Naples, and finally 
Venice. It was in Italy that I had my first encounter with making coffee at home. You see, we 
stayed in various AirBnbs all throughout the country, and there was one thing in common with 
all of them: They had this quaint little device called a "Mokapot". 

![a mokapot](/blog-images/coffee/mokapot.webp)
*a Mokapot in every household in Italy*

I was absolutely fascinated with the mokapot, and the idea that I could just make coffee at home
was so enticing. I ended up using the mokapot almost everyday and learnt what were the tips and tricks 
to get a better brew out of it. I was really tempted to buy a mokapot, especially since we were in Italy, 
but I decided to hold off so that I could make a better decision when I got back to Singapore. 
Fast forward to after exchange and a lot of mulling and over-analysing the decision, I decided that I would 
buy a Picopresso.
